Hove Beach

Ample (paid) parking on the main seafront road provides access to the long stretch of pebbly beach to the West of Brighton's West Pier.

Easy to launch and best on mid-low tide with light winds. Exposed to S/SW winds, which will make conditions a little messy.
Be mindful of underwater debris if paddling near the now derelict West Pier.

The facilities around the i360 or Rockwater near the lagoon offer plenty of post-paddle refreshment opportunity!

River Adur

This River runs for approximately 20 miles and gives it's name to the Adur district of West Sussex.

Routes can start from the Shoreham area or further up the river from the Ladywell Corner car park on Coombes Road.
Time your paddle to hit high tide around Upper Beeding before returning on the outgoing tide.
Paddle times approximately 1.5hrs (from Shoreham) or 1hr from Coombes Road.

River Ouse

Running from Lower Beeding to Newhaven, this is one of the larger Rivers in the Sussex counties.

A popular route is to enter the water at Southease slipway, where it is possible to park, 2hrs before high-tide at Newhaven and paddle North to Lewes.
The route offers stunning views across the South Downs and there is opportunity to get off at Lewes for a lunch stop before returning.

Use caution when embarking from Southease as the water can be a little turbulent.
